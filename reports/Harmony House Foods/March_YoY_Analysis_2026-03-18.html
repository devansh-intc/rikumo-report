2. The Real Issue: Why Is Top Line Down When Platform ROAS Looks Good?

This is the #1 question to answer on the client call.

Platform ROAS looks healthy — Google at 4.9x, Facebook at 4.92x, blended at 4.77x. So why is total Shopify revenue down 28.8% YoY?

Root Cause Analysis

  1. The Organic/Direct Baseline Has Eroded
    In 2025, platforms claimed only $28,105.68 in conversion value for the full month, but total Shopify sales were $117,003.05. That means $88,897.37 (76.0%) came from organic, direct, email, and other non-ad channels. The high MER of 14.8x existed because organic was doing the heavy lifting and ads were a small supplement.

    In 2026, the business’s organic baseline has continued its multi-year decline (annual revenue: $1.81M in 2021 → $1.02M in 2025 — down 44%). Scaling paid ads cannot reverse this structural decline. More ad spend just masks it.
  2. Facebook Is the Biggest Spend Line But Has Low Incrementality
    Facebook consumes 60.6% of total ad budget ($5,331.02 of $8,799.43).
    • FB self-reports $26,247.59 in conversion value — a 4.92x ROAS. Looks great.
    • But Shopify UTM last-click attributes only $12,047.36 to all Facebook/Instagram paid sources — an effective ROAS of just 2.26x.
    • The gap ($14,200.23) is FB claiming credit for view-through conversions and assisted clicks that Shopify attributes to direct/organic.
    • In 2025, FB spend was only $153 for the entire month. Those “FB-attributed” customers were buying anyway through organic search, direct, and email.
  3. Missing Promotional Event
    March 12, 2025 had a flash sale: 89 orders, $13,252.52 in sales, $3,391.45 in discounts. This single day = 17.5% of all March 1–17 2025 sales. No equivalent promotion has been run in 2026.
  4. Lower Discount Aggressiveness
    2025 (Mar 1–17) discount rate: 12.8% of gross sales
    2026 (Mar 1–17) discount rate: 8.0% of gross sales
    Less discounting = better margins per order, but fewer total orders and lower top-line volume.
Bottom Line for the Client: Platform ROAS is a measure of efficiency within the paid channel — and that IS good. But MER measures how much total revenue you generate per ad dollar. The MER drop from 14.8x to 6.1x means the incremental revenue from scaling ads (especially Facebook) is much lower than expected, because a large portion of “ad-attributed” purchases would have happened organically anyway. The top line is down because organic demand has weakened and aggressive FB spend isn’t creating enough new demand to compensate.

7. Recommendations & Action Plan

1. Cut Facebook Daily Budget Immediately ($313.59/day → $100–150/day)
Shift to retargeting-only (warm audiences, cart abandoners, past purchasers). Kill prospecting/cold campaigns. This saves ~$150–200/day ($2,100.00–$2,800.00 for remaining 14 days). The UTM data shows FB last-click ROAS is only 2.26x — the platform is over-reporting by 54.1%.
2. Scale Google PMax Budget ($190/day → $250–300/day)
PMax is budget-limited at 4.88x ROAS — this is the highest-efficiency paid channel. Increasing budget should capture incremental Shopping traffic. Branded Search (6.85x ROAS) should remain as-is. Estimated incremental: $60–110/day extra spend × ~4x ROAS = $240–440/day additional revenue.
3. Launch a Flash Sale / Promotion (March 19–22)
Last year’s March 12 promo generated $13,252.52 in a single day with 89 orders. Run a 48–72hr flash sale (20–25% off best sellers) promoted via Klaviyo email blast + social. Target: $8,000–15,000 in incremental sales. This is the single biggest lever to close the gap.
4. Increase Email Frequency (Klaviyo)
Email is driving 21 orders / $3,875.77 in total sales at zero ad cost (infinite ROI). This is the most efficient channel. Send 2–3 additional blasts this week: flash sale announcement, abandoned cart recovery push, “best sellers of March” curated collection. Each blast can drive $500–2,000 in incremental sales.
5. Set Realistic Expectations on 15% Target
The 15% growth target ($134,553.51) needs $5,761.63/day for the remaining 14 days — that’s 1.8x the current daily average of $3,170.04. This is only achievable with a major promotional event. A realistic target: come within 10–15% of last year’s $117,003.05.
6. Reframe the Narrative for the Client
  • Last year’s March was inflated by a one-time flash sale ($13,252.52 on Mar 12). Excl. that, real gap is ~17.7% not ~28.8%.
  • Google Ads efficiency has improved (ROAS 3.62x → 4.91x). We’re spending smarter on Google.
  • We’ve identified FB as the budget drain and are course-correcting now.
  • Immediate plan: flash sale + email push + FB budget reallocation = potential $10K–20K uplift before month-end.
  • Long-term: annual revenue has declined 44% since 2021. Paid media optimizes within that reality — we need to also discuss SEO, retention, and product strategy.
