2. Root Cause: Why Is Revenue Down When Orders Are Flat?

This is the #1 question to answer on the client call.

Orders are nearly identical (417 in 2025 vs 411 in 2026 — only 1.4% difference). The revenue gap is driven by a shift in customer mix: more new customers (lower AOV) and fewer returning customers compared to last year.

Revenue Lost to AOV Drop — $7,983.78 (if AOV had stayed at $181.57, revenue would be $74,625.37)
Bottom Line — This Is a Customer Mix Effect:

2025 strategy: Heavy discounts (12.8% discount rate), 227 returning / 190 new customers. Returning customers drove the majority of orders and lifted the blended AOV.

2026 strategy: Full-price new customer focus (8.0% discount rate), 146 returning / 265 new customers. New customers naturally have lower AOV ($119.73) vs. returning customers ($221.34). Returning customer AOV remains strong — that cohort has not weakened.

This is a healthy strategic shift — growing the customer base at full price will pay off in future repeat purchases.

6. Recommendations & Action Plan

1. Launch Sale & Offer Ads on Facebook
Facebook is already driving 100 UTM-attributed orders. Lean into this channel by launching dedicated sale/offer creative (bundle deals, seasonal promotions, limited-time offers). This can increase conversion rates and order volume without raising the budget ceiling. Target: increase FB order volume by 20–30% through better-converting creative.
2. Promote High-AOV Products (Samplers, Kits & Bundles)
The AOV drop is the core revenue issue. Push high-AOV products in email campaigns and Facebook ads:
  • Vegetable Sampler (15 ZIP) — #1 seller at $4,585.60
  • Deluxe Sampler (32 ZIP), Pantry Stuffers, Family Packs — all $100+ AOV products
  • Create a “Best Value Bundles” featured collection on the homepage
Goal: bring AOV back toward $181.57 (from current $162.14).
3. Optimize Google PMax for Better ROAS (Keep Budget As-Is)
PMax is running at 4.88x ROAS on $190/day — solid performance. Rather than scaling budget, focus on optimizing the existing account: refine audience signals, improve product feed, and test asset groups. Total ad spend should stay under $14,000/month. Branded Search (6.85x ROAS) should remain as-is.
4. Increase Email Frequency (Klaviyo)
Email is driving 21 orders / $3,875.77 in total sales at zero ad cost (infinite ROI). Send 2–3 additional blasts this week: best sellers of March, abandoned cart recovery push, seasonal “spring pantry refresh” collection. Each blast can drive $500–2,000 in incremental sales.
5. Set Realistic Expectations on 15% Target
The 15% growth target ($134,553.51) needs $4,850.85/day for the remaining 14 days — that’s 1.2x the current daily average of $3,920.09. With the $14K ad spend cap and no flash sale, the focus should be on maximizing efficiency across email, Facebook offer ads, and Google PMax optimization. A realistic target: match or come within 5–10% of last year’s $117,003.05.
