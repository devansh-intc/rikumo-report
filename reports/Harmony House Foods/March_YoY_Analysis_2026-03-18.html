7. What's Driving the Gap?

Root Causes

  1. Missing Promotional Event: March 12, 2025's flash sale/promo generated $13,252.52 (89 orders, $3,391.45 in discounts). No equivalent event has occurred in 2026. This single day accounts for ~17.5% of March 1–17 2025 sales.
  2. Organic/Direct Traffic Decline: The "Direct / Organic / Unattributed" channel drives 60.7% of 2026 sales. Without 2025 UTM data for comparison, we can't measure the exact decline, but the overall business is trending down YoY even outside of paid channels.
  3. Facebook Spend Scaling Too Aggressively: FB spend went from $153/month to ~$9,721/month (projected). The incremental revenue is not keeping pace — the marginal ROAS on FB is poor.
  4. Lower Discount Aggressiveness in 2026: 2025 (Mar 1–17) discount rate: 12.8% | 2026 (Mar 1–17) discount rate: 8.0%. Lower discounts = better margins but fewer orders.
  5. Underlying Business Trend: Annual revenue has declined every year since 2021 ($1.81M → $1.02M in 2025, per annual data). This is a structural headwind — paid media alone cannot reverse a 44% organic decline.

8. Recommendations for the Client Call

1. Run a Flash Sale / Promotion ASAP (March 19–22)
Last year's March 12 promo generated $13,252 in a single day with 89 orders. A similar event could close much of the gap. Recommend a 48–72 hour flash sale (20–25% off popular items) promoted via email + social. This alone could add $8,000–15,000 in incremental revenue.
2. Scale Down Facebook Ads Immediately
Cut FB daily budget from ~$314/day to $100–150/day (retargeting only). FB is burning $314/day with UTM-attributed ROAS of only 2.26x. Reallocate savings to Google PMax and email campaigns. Potential savings: ~$2,500–3,000 for the rest of March.
3. Increase Google PMax Budget
The PMax campaign has 4.88x ROAS and is budget-limited at $190/day. Increase to $250–300/day. This is the highest-efficiency paid channel. The branded search campaign is already efficient at 6.85x ROAS — keep as-is.
4. Double Down on Email (Klaviyo)
Email is attributing 21 orders / $3,875.77 in total sales with zero ad cost (infinite ROI). Send 2–3 additional campaigns this week: flash sale announcement, abandoned cart push, and a "best sellers" feature. Each email blast can drive $500–2,000 in incremental sales.
5. Set Realistic Expectations on the 15% Target
The 15% YoY growth target ($134,553.51) requires $5,761.63/day for the remaining 14 days — that's 1.8x the current daily average. This is only achievable with a major promotional event. A more realistic target given current trends: match or come within 10–15% of last year's $117,003.05.
6. Reframe the Narrative for the Client
Key talking points:
  • Last year's March was inflated by a massive single-day promo ($13,252.52 on Mar 12). Excluding that, the real gap is ~17.7% not ~28.8%.
  • Google Ads efficiency has improved — ROAS up from 3.62x to 4.91x. We're spending smarter.
  • We've identified FB as the budget drain and are course-correcting now.
  • Immediate action plan: flash sale + email push + FB budget reallocation can recover $10,000–20,000 before month-end.
  • The business has a structural organic decline (revenue down 44% since 2021). Paid media optimizes within that reality — we need to also discuss SEO, retention, and product strategy.
