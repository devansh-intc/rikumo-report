Google Ads Platform vs Shopify Attribution

Understanding the ROAS gap
Google-Reported Conversion Value vs UTM-Tracked CPC Revenue
What This Means

Google reports an average 43.8x ROAS for 2025. That number is inflated because Google attributes conversions broadly — including view-through conversions, cross-device, and users who clicked an ad but purchased later through another channel.

Shopify UTM shows 4.72x ROAS — only counting purchases where the user's last click before buying was a Google CPC ad. This is the most conservative measure.

The real ROAS is likely 6x–15x, somewhere between these two figures. Google Ads is clearly driving significant brand awareness and consideration that results in purchases through other channels (direct, email, etc.).

Bottom line: Even at the most conservative 4.72x measurement, every dollar spent on Google Ads generates $4.72 in tracked revenue. The true contribution including assisted conversions is much higher.

Campaign Optimization — Fix Before Scaling

Account consolidation must happen before any budget increase
Critical: Simply adding budget to the current account structure will not produce optimal results. The issues below must be addressed first. Expect 2–4 weeks of restructuring before scaling.
1
Consolidate 5 Campaigns → 2 Campaigns
Currently running 5 campaigns: HomeGarden ($12–$18/day), BathBeauty ($12–$18/day), Leads ($3/day), GeneralShopping ($3/day), and CanadaTest ($10–$14/day). Action plan:
• Pause CanadaTest immediately (8.1x ROAS vs ~50x for US — see Campaign Deep Dive above)
• Merge HomeGarden, BathBeauty, Leads, and GeneralShopping into 2 campaigns: Brand + Non-Brand
• Use ad groups for category segmentation (Home, Bath, Tableware, Tea) instead of separate campaigns
• This concentrates the full budget into fewer, data-rich campaigns and frees up ~$350/month from Canada
2
Fix Bidding Strategy — Add Targets
All campaigns are running Maximize Conversions with no target set. Without a target, Google will spend the full daily budget regardless of efficiency. Fix:
• After consolidation, switch to Target ROAS (start at 400%–500%) on Search campaigns
• For Pmax: use Maximize Conversion Value with a Target ROAS of 300% initially
• Let the algorithm stabilize for 2–3 weeks after setting targets before changing anything else
• Monitor impression share — if it drops significantly, the target is too aggressive
3
Restructure Pmax ($3/Day → Proper Budget)
The current $3/day Pmax budget generates ~200 clicks/month — not enough data for the algorithm to learn. After Search consolidation:
• Increase Pmax to $10–$15/day ($300–$500/month) as part of total budget
• Build 3 asset groups: Kitchenware & Ceramics, Tea & Food, Home Décor & Bath
• Upload 15+ lifestyle images, 5 headlines, 5 descriptions per asset group
• Add audience signals: Japanese culture, home décor, tea enthusiasts, competitor URLs
• Run for 4–6 weeks minimum before evaluating performance
4
Quick Wins Within Current Budget
While restructuring, extract more value from the existing spend:
• Review search terms report — add negative keywords for irrelevant queries
• Check ad scheduling — optimize for peak conversion hours
• Review device performance — adjust bids for mobile vs desktop
• Audit landing pages — ensure ads link to relevant category pages, not homepage
• Add sitelink and callout extensions if not already present
