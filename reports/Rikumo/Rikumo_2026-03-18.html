The $0.33 CPC in Feb 2026 proves there's cheap, untapped inventory available.

Google Ads Platform vs Shopify Attribution

Understanding the ROAS gap
Google-Reported Conversion Value vs UTM-Tracked CPC Revenue
What This Means

Google reports an average 43.8x ROAS for 2025. That number is inflated because Google attributes conversions broadly — including view-through conversions, cross-device, and users who clicked an ad but purchased later through another channel.

Shopify UTM shows 4.72x ROAS — only counting purchases where the user's last click before buying was a Google CPC ad. This is the most conservative measure.

The real ROAS is likely 6x–15x, somewhere between these two figures. Google Ads is clearly driving significant brand awareness and consideration that results in purchases through other channels (direct, email, etc.).

Bottom line: Even at the most conservative 4.72x measurement, every dollar spent on Google Ads generates $4.72 in tracked revenue. The true contribution including assisted conversions is much higher.

Key Insights & Action Items

Scale — Immediate Opportunities
Google Search Ads Are Under-Funded
At $1,120/month with 4.72x ROAS, this account is leaving money on the table. The Jan 2026 proof (doubled spend, ROAS held) confirms there's room to scale to $3K–$5K/month without efficiency loss.
Launch Real Pmax Campaigns
$67 in Feb 2026 isn't a test — it's barely a pulse. Pmax at $1K–$2K/month with proper asset groups for Rikumo's beautiful product imagery would unlock Shopping, YouTube, and Discovery inventory at $0.33 CPC.
Holiday Season Amplification
Nov & Dec saw 6.4x–6.6x ROAS on just $1,568–$1,697 spend. Doubling or tripling holiday budget to $5K–$8K/month during Q4 would capture significantly more holiday demand.
Watch — Monitor Closely
Order Volume Is Declining YoY
Jan orders dropped -13.3% and Feb dropped -17.6% YoY. AOV is up (+14–19%), offsetting most of the decline in revenue, but fewer customers is a warning signal. Paid ads can help reverse this trend.
March 2025 Was an Outlier
March showed only 1.4x UTM ROAS — the worst month by far. With higher spend ($1,337) but only $1,923 in CPC revenue, something was off. Investigate whether campaign settings, keyword targeting, or landing pages changed that month.
Email Drives 14% but Is It Optimized?
Klaviyo newsletters + flows + back-in-stock emails drove $147K in 2025. Jan 2026 saw a massive jump to $32K (vs $25K in Jan 2025). Ensure email isn't cannibalizing potential ad conversions — coordinate ad and email timing.
Act Now — Critical
75% Revenue Has Zero Attribution
Three-quarters of revenue shows no UTM source. This means most buyers arrive directly or through organic channels. While great for margins, it's a vulnerability — any SEO algorithm change or brand-awareness dip could tank revenue with no paid safety net.
No Spend on Social Ads
Pinterest drives organic social sales, but there's zero paid social budget. For a visual, lifestyle brand like Rikumo, Instagram and Pinterest paid campaigns could be highly effective. Even $500–$1K/month in testing would provide data.
Google Shopping Feed Under-Leveraged
Free product_sync listings drive $24K/year — but paid Shopping (via Pmax) would significantly amplify this. Ensure the Merchant Center feed has optimized titles, descriptions, GTINs, and product types for maximum visibility.

Strategic Recommendations

Prioritized action plan for scaling Rikumo's paid acquisition
1
Scale Google Search to $3,000/Month (Phase 1)
Increase the monthly Search budget from $1,120 to $3,000 immediately. The January 2026 data proves that doubling spend maintained 4.5x ROAS. At $3K/month, expect $12K+ in monthly CPC revenue vs the current $5.3K. Focus additional budget on:
• Expanding keyword coverage (long-tail Japanese homeware terms)
• Competitive conquest campaigns (target competitor brand searches)
• Category-specific campaigns (tea, ceramics, kitchen, bath)
2
Launch Proper Pmax Campaigns ($1,000–$2,000/Month)
The $68 "test" in Feb 2026 doesn't qualify as a real experiment. Allocate $1K–$2K/month with:
• 3 asset groups: Kitchenware & Ceramics, Tea & Food, Home Décor & Bath
• 15+ high-quality lifestyle images per asset group
• 5 headlines + 5 descriptions highlighting Japanese craftsmanship
• Audience signals: Japanese culture enthusiasts, home décor shoppers, tea lovers
• Run for 6 weeks minimum before evaluating
3
Optimize the Same Budget First (Quick Wins)
Before adding budget, ensure the existing $1,120 is spent optimally:
• Review search terms report — are there wasted clicks on irrelevant queries?
• Add negative keywords aggressively (e.g., "free," "DIY," "wholesale," "cheap")
• Check ad scheduling — are ads running 24/7 or optimized for peak conversion hours?
• Review device bids — mobile vs desktop performance may vary significantly
• Audit landing pages — ensure ads link to relevant category/product pages, not just homepage
4
Build a Q4 / Holiday Scaling Plan
Nov & Dec 2025 delivered $10K+ in CPC revenue each at 6.4–6.6x ROAS on just $1,568–$1,697 spend. For Q4 2026, plan to:
• Increase budget to $5,000–$8,000/month for Oct–Dec
• Launch holiday-specific campaigns and ad copy
• Create gift-guide landing pages for Pmax asset groups
• Start ramping budget in September to build campaign history
• Estimated Q4 impact: +$40K–$60K in additional holiday revenue
5
Test Paid Social ($500–$1,000/Month)
Rikumo's aesthetic products are perfect for visual platforms. Pinterest organic already drives sales. Start testing with:
• Pinterest Ads: Product pins + collection ads targeting home décor and Japanese culture interests
• Instagram/Meta Ads: Lifestyle imagery, influencer UGC, retargeting website visitors
• Budget: $500–$1K/month split across platforms
• Focus on top-of-funnel awareness + retargeting warm audiences
6
Diversify Away from 75% Unattributed Revenue
Relying on organic/direct traffic for three-quarters of revenue is a vulnerability. To reduce risk:
• Scale paid channels to 15–20% of revenue (from current 6%)
• Invest in SEO content — Japanese lifestyle blog, product guides, craft stories
• Build email list aggressively — email already drives 14% of revenue and is highly efficient
• Track all touchpoints — ensure UTM parameters are on every link from every channel
• Goal: reduce unattributed revenue from 75% to under 50% within 12 months
