The MER improvement is real — efficiency is up

Revenue fell 42% but ad spend fell 65%. Every dollar spent on ads now generates $5.35 in Shopify revenue vs $3.23 last year. The business is more efficient, not less. Organic, email, and returning customers are contributing more.

Revenue decline is primarily a spend reduction story

💡

The 2025 discount model was unsustainable

Giving 45–73% discounts to convert customers is effectively buying revenue. In 2025, $77K was given away in discounts on $170K of gross sales. The aggressive discounting inflated order counts (especially single-item purchases of Healer, Defend, Reishi) but at razor-thin or negative margins. Moving to subscriptions is strategically correct — the question is execution.

⚠️

Subscription conversion needs a new offer architecture

The "+3 Lion's Mane Special (53% savings)" dropped from 34 orders to 8 — an 77% collapse — because it was the most popular one-time-purchase deal. If this offer now requires subscription, the friction is too high for cold ad traffic. Consider a hybrid: one-time purchase at a moderate discount, with subscription offering even bigger ongoing savings (subscribe & save upsell post-purchase).

✅

"Pulse" Shiitake and "50% Off Bundle" are growing

Shiitake went from 9 to 30 orders (+288%). The "50% Off Most Potent" bundle went from 8 to 15 orders (+56%). These products are resonating under the new model. They likely represent customers who see genuine value without needing the extreme multi-buy discount to commit. Worth increasing ad investment behind these.

⚠️

Returns up 135% — needs immediate monitoring

Returns grew from $1,256 to $2,952. Most returns in 2026 are concentrated in Lion's Mane ($1,534) and multiple secondary products ($29.50–$88.50 each). In a subscription model, early returns often signal customers who subscribed for the discount but didn't intend to continue. Track monthly return rate as subscriptions mature.

PV-to-ATC rate collapsed: 37% → 8.6%

In 2025, 37% of page viewers added to cart. In 2026, only 8.6% do. Impressions are down, clicks are up, but cart adds have plummeted. This is the clearest signal that the landing page / offer is not converting. The subscription commitment or price point is creating a major drop-off between viewing the product and adding it to cart.

💡

ATC-to-Purchase improved: 23% → 18% (similar)

Customers who do add to cart are completing purchase at a similar rate (23% → 18%). The problem is getting people to the cart — not checkout abandonment. This means the fix is at the offer/product page level, not at the checkout or cart level. Test a clearer subscription value prop and consider displaying the long-term savings more prominently.

Strategic Recommendations

Prioritized action plan based on the data analysis above

1

Fix the Facebook Funnel Before Scaling

The PV-to-ATC rate (8.6%) is the critical bottleneck. Until this recovers to 20%+, more Facebook spend will just amplify waste.

  • A/B test a hybrid offer: one-time purchase at 25–30% off vs subscribe & save 45%
  • Make subscription value prop (total savings, flexibility, cancel anytime) front-and-center on the product page
  • Test a trial/starter pack at a lower price point to reduce first-purchase friction
2

Scale Google Ads First

Google ROAS improved YoY (1.30x → 1.52x) despite 75% spend reduction. It's performing better per dollar and has room to grow.

  • Increase Google budget by $500–1,000/day incrementally over 2 weeks
  • Audit keyword Quality Scores — CPC nearly doubled, suggesting score degradation
  • Add branded search campaigns to capture subscription-intent searches
  • Target ROAS: 2.0x before adding further budget
3

Defend Lion's Mane Revenue

The flagship product lost 47.5% in net sales (615 → 396 orders). It's still 63% of total revenue but the decline is steep.

  • Test a "starter subscription" at $49–59 for one bottle (lower commitment)
  • Launch a dedicated retargeting campaign for page visitors who didn't add to cart
  • Create a comparison creative: cost per day with subscription vs one-time
4

Invest Behind "Pulse" Shiitake & 50% Bundle

These two products grew strongly under the new model (+288% and +56%). They're proof the subscription offer can work for the right products.

  • Allocate 10–15% of Facebook budget to Pulse Shiitake creative
  • Create bundle-specific ads for the 50% Off Most Potent bundle
  • Test upselling Pulse to existing Lion's Mane subscribers via email
5

Monitor & Address Rising Returns

Returns up 135% ($1,256 → $2,952) is an early warning sign. In subscription models, unresolved returns become churn and negative reviews.

  • Track monthly return rate as Q1 subscription cohort matures
  • Add a post-purchase email sequence (Days 7, 14, 21) to educate on product benefits and prevent buyer's remorse
  • Survey returners: understand if it's quality, price, or subscription friction
6

Leverage the High MER to Fund Growth

MER of 5.35x means the business is generating $5.35 for every $1 of ad spend — mostly from organic + subscribers. This is a strong foundation.

  • Use the healthy MER as justification to re-invest in paid: target $15–20K/month total spend
  • Set a hard floor: if MER drops below 4x, reduce spend immediately
  • Consider email/SMS campaigns to existing subscribers to increase LTV and reduce dependence on paid acquisition
